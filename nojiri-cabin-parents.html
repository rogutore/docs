01 — The Heart of the Matter

What This Is Really About

Before we get into plans and numbers, we want to be clear about what's actually at stake here. It's not really about a building.

It's about time. Time that passes whether we use it or not. Time with you. Time with the grandkids while they're still young. Time as a family, together, in a place that has meant so much to so many of us.

It's about presence. The difference between a cabin that exists someday and a cabin that holds us now—with all the noise and mess and joy that comes with everyone actually being there.

The Verses We Chose

We opened with Proverbs 15:22 because we believe this: we succeed when we seek counsel together. That's why we're bringing this to you—not to present a decision, but to ask for your wisdom.

And we'll close with Philippians 2:3-4, because that's the spirit we want to hold: not looking to our own interests, but to the interests of others. This isn't about any of us getting our way. It's about finding a path that honors everyone.

What We're Really Weighing

Buildings can be perfected. Square footage can be optimized. Insulation specs can be debated endlessly.

But the years when grandparents and grandchildren overlap? When the older kids are still around? When everyone wants to be together in one place? Those years are finite. They don't wait for permits or perfect plans.

That's the tension underneath everything else. And we don't think there's a formula that resolves it. Only wisdom, prayer, and family.

Thank you for being the kind of parents worth building something with—not just for.

02 — Where Things Stand

How We Got Here

You know most of this already. But for the sake of clarity, here's a brief summary of the path that led to the current impasse.

Original Plan Submitted

A new-build design was developed and presented to the neighbors for approval.

Neighbor Rejection

Height was capped at 6.5 meters—making much of the second floor/loft unviable as real living space.

Plans Changed, Costs Didn't

The design was compromised, but the price stayed high: ¥45–55 million.

Family Consensus

We said: we will not let you pay that much for a plan that doesn't fully serve you. If that's where we are, we'd rather let it go.

New Direction

The kids should pay for everything. But at that price point, we can't afford a new build.

Impasse

Without a new path, we face the real possibility of years passing with no progress.

Where We Stand

We refuse to let you pay a large sum for something that doesn't truly serve you. That's the line we've drawn—not out of pride, but out of love. If we can't find a path that makes sense for everyone, we'd rather wait.

Thank you for your patience through all of this. We know it hasn't been easy to watch the plans shift and stall. We're grateful you're still willing to think through it with us.

03 — The Real Question

What Are We Actually Deciding?

This decision is no longer about square meters, insulation specs, or architectural perfection.

It's About

Who gets to enjoy the cabin. When they get to enjoy it. And at what cost—not just financial, but relational and emotional.

The Scenario We Want to Avoid

A future where we wait until you no longer want to make the trip, until the kids have scattered, until the window has closed—and then we build the "perfect" cabin with your money.

That cabin wouldn't be perfect at all. It would be empty of the people and moments that give it meaning.

The Core Question

If we put "bigger, better cabin later" on one side of a scale, and "shared years together now" on the other—how should we weigh them?

That's what we're asking you to help us think through.

04 — The Options

Four Paths Forward

Each option has trade-offs. None is perfect. We're not advocating for any one of them—we want to lay them out so we can discuss together.

Option A — Renovation

Kids only

Concept

Renovate the existing structure. Same footprint, squared-off back side. Prioritize safety, warmth, and cost containment.

What's Included

  • Jack up and level the building
  • Repair or add foundation stilts
  • Replace all rotten beams and joists
  • Full insulation: floors, walls, ceilings (~9cm)
  • Double-pane windows
  • Water and sewage

Limitations

  • No budget for roof work
  • Much smaller than original plan
  • Not suitable for family Christmas or snow camp
  • Limited hosting capacity

Financially clean and low risk—but emotionally constrained. A cabin, but not the gathering place we imagined.

Option B — Simplified New Build
Conditional

¥30M (hard cap)

Parents + Kids chip in

Concept

A simplified new build under the negotiated constraints. Almost half the cost of the original plan. Deck absorbed into interior space. Lower specs than the original dream, but still warm, code-compliant, brand new, and highly usable.

Why This Is Back in the Conversation

Several factors have shifted since we first set this aside: the significantly lower cost (nearly half), the possibility that Noah's family may get NLA membership (which would mean one less family needing space, making a smaller cabin more viable), and time spent reconsidering the concerns different family members have raised. Together, these changes prompted us to take another look.

Key Feature

  • You would have your own private room. This is firm. A space that is truly yours.

Layout Concept

  • First floor: 2–3 simple rooms, including your room
  • Upper level: large open loft—everyone sleeps together, no individual rooms
  • Lakeside: open vertical space for scale without violating height rules

Our Promise

  • Kids do everything possible to contribute and chip in
  • Kids will make sure you are taken care of no matter what
  • Miura house money hack or other mechanisms if needed
  • No scenario where you are left vulnerable

A simpler cabin—but one where you can actually stay, be present, and make memories with kids and grandkids while the window is open.

Option B Is Only Viable If...

  • You agree with the thesis. That trading "perfect later" for "meaningful now" makes sense—that the years of presence are worth more than the specs we'd sacrifice.
  • The numbers make sense to you. Not just affordable, but genuinely worth it in your eyes.
  • Everyone chips in. This isn't you funding our dream. It's all of us building something together.

No one wants it any other way. If this doesn't sit right with you, we don't want it either.

05 — The Core Tradeoff

What Are We Really Weighing?

Underneath all the specs and budgets, there's a simpler question.

"Perfect Later"

  • Bigger, better cabin
  • Built when circumstances align
  • Possibly when you're older
  • Possibly after kids have moved on
  • When desire may be lower

"Meaningful Now"

  • Smaller, simpler cabin
  • Built while it still matters
  • With you there
  • With kids and grandkids
  • Memories that actually happen

Which one is truly more "perfect"?

We don't have the answer. We're asking you to help us find it.

06 — Additional Context

A Few Other Things Worth Knowing

Noah may become a Nojiri Lake Association member this summer. If that happens, he would purchase his own separate cabin within the NLA community.

What this means for the family cabin:

  • One less family needing space

Whatever we decide, we will not let this consume your resources or leave you vulnerable. Your safety, security, and dignity matter more than any cabin. The "Miura house money hack" and other mechanisms exist to ensure that—no matter what happens—you are protected.

Our Guiding Principle

Harmony over optimization. The goal is not to find the theoretically best cabin. It's to find a path we can all walk together—without resentment, without regret, without anyone feeling unseen.

Thank you for the years you've already poured into this family. Whatever we build—or don't build—that's the foundation that matters most.
