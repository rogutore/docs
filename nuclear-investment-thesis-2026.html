02 — Sector Thesis

The Nuclear Renaissance: Structural Drivers

This isn't a trade—it's a thesis built on irreversible structural changes in energy demand, geopolitics, and policy. Three converging forces make nuclear the highest-conviction early-cycle sector for the next decade.

Driver 1: AI Data Center Demand

Large language models and AI infrastructure require 24/7 baseload power that renewables cannot reliably provide. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon) are signing unprecedented nuclear power agreements:

  • Microsoft + Constellation (Sept 2024): 20-year PPA to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1, the first restart of a decommissioned US nuclear plant
  • Amazon + Talen Energy (March 2024): $650M acquisition of a data center campus directly connected to Susquehanna nuclear plant
  • Google + Kairos Power (Oct 2024): Agreement to deploy small modular reactors for data center power by 2030

Driver 2: Russian Supply Chain Disruption

Russia controls approximately 44% of global uranium enrichment capacity. Western sanctions and supply chain reshoring are creating structural shortages:

Milestone Date Impact
ADVANCE Act signed July 2024 $2.7B for domestic enrichment
Russian LEU ban enacted August 2024 Waivers through 2027
Waiver expiration January 2028 Full Russian import ban
Centrus HALEU production 2029 First US HALEU at scale

Driver 3: Policy Tailwinds

For the first time in decades, nuclear energy has bipartisan political support and active policy acceleration:

  • Trump Executive Orders (Jan 2025): Directive to quadruple US nuclear capacity, streamline NRC permitting
  • COP28 Declaration: 22 nations committed to tripling nuclear capacity by 2050
  • DOE Funding: $6.1B allocated for domestic fuel cycle, advanced reactors
  • Japan Restarts: Policy reversal enabling reactor restarts post-Fukushima

Early-Cycle Confirmation

Nuclear scores 24/25 on our sector evaluation framework—the highest of 8 early-cycle sectors analyzed. Policy is enacted, money is committed, but less than 10% of required capital has been deployed. We are in the first inning of a multi-decade buildout.

04 — Portfolio Structure

The 30/35/40 Allocation

The portfolio is structured across three tiers designed to balance capital preservation with asymmetric upside. This is not a "nuclear ETF with extra steps"—it's a thesis-driven allocation that justifies the research effort through concentrated exposure to the highest-conviction opportunities.

Why Tiered Allocation?

A portfolio of only large-cap nuclear names (CCJ, CEG) would produce safe but capped returns—essentially market performance plus a nuclear premium. To justify front-loading annual research and capture 3-10x outcomes, the portfolio intentionally overweights asymmetric positions with real fundamentals.
